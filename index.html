Runtime Journey

I grew up not particularly loving CS — in fact, I used to hate it in school. Then in Class 11 and 12, I took CS and it crept into my world slowly. I landed in BTech almost by default — "let's just take Computer Science" — and spent the first phase of it trying everything: fullstack development, software engineering, building apps. None of it stuck the way I expected.

Then I touched data. EDA on a messy dataset, a model that actually predicted something real, a dashboard that made a business insight visible — and suddenly the whole field made sense to me. I didn't just find a career path. I found the thing I was good at and liked being good at.

Now I am a Dean's List student with a 9.32 CGPA, who has interned in Data Science, built ML models for real-world problems, and who writes on Medium to make sense of what I learn.
